Taylor Swift

Timeline of Taylor's career

  • Taylor Alison Swift was born on December 13, 1989 in West Reading, PA
  • June 2006 – releases first single titled “Our Song”
  • October 2006 – releases first album titled “Taylor Swift”
  • 2007 – The Horizon Award for best new artist
  • 2008 – releases second album titled “Fearless”
  • 2009 – First tour as a headliner “the Fearless Tour” Album of the year ACM Award (“Fearless”) & Video Music Award for best female video
  • 2010 – Wins 4 Grammys, including for album of the year, Releases third album titled “Speak Now”
  • 2011 – CMA entertainer of the year Award
  • 2012 – Fourth album: “Red”
  • 2014 – Fifth album: “1989”, Album of the year Grammy Award for “1989”
  • 2017 – Sixth album: “Reputation”
  • 2019 – Seventh album: “Lover”
  • 2020 – Eight and ninth albums: “Folklore” & “Evermore”, Taylor begins re-recording the albums she doesn’t own, Album of the year Grammy for “Folklore”
  • 2021 – Rerelease of her 2nd & 4th albums “Fearless (Taylor’s Version)” & “Red (Taylor’s Version)”
  • 2022 – Tenth studio album: “Midnights”
  • 2023 – The Eras concert tour begins, Rerelease of her 3rd & 5th albums “Speak Now (Taylor’s Version)” & “1989 (Taylor’s Version)”, MTV Video Music Awards: 9 wins
  • 2024 – Album of the year Grammy award for “Midnights”, Eleventh studio album: "The Tortured Poets Department"

Taylor Swift is the most-awarded artist of the:

  • American Music Awards (40)
  • Billboard Music Awards (39)
  • MTV Video Music Awards (30)
  • iHeartRadio Music Awards (25)
She also holds the:
  • Most Album of the Year wins at the Grammy awards (4)
  • Most Video of the Year wins at the MTV Video Music Awards (5)
  • Most awarded Global Recording Artist of the Year by the International Federation of the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (4)
Taylor was included in the Time 100 most influential people list three times (2009, 2015, 2019), was the first woman to be named Time’s Person of the year twice (2017, 2023), and is the first entertainer to ever be named Person of the year.
